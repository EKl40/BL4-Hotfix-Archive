Version 1.7 - May 28, 2026

Today, we released our second Raid Boss fight featuring Subjugator and Thol the Invincible! Additionally, we added UVHM7, a Shared Progression Toggle, enhanced several Vault Hunters’ skills, updated weapon interactions, and more. If that’s not enough to fuel your hunt, a number of brand-new Pearlescents are now obtainable through any loot source that provides weapons.

In this update, we also made significant strides to provide you with a more consistent combat experience. Some key changes are: 

    Reworked XP rewards from some of our toughest challenges. 
    A tune-up to C4SH’s skills.
    Updates to the Bod Shotgun and Torgue Sticky projectiles

New Free Content*
Raid Boss 2: Subjugator and Thol the Invincible

Subjugator and Thol the Invincible have long been languishing in their service to the Timekeeper. They’ve spent ages isolated on an island off the coast of the Fadefields, waiting for a worthy challenger to come along: you, Vault Hunter. 

This encounter starts with you facing off against both bosses at the same time, but as one dies, you’ll be taken to the real boss battle. We’ll be testing your skills and endurance in this new fight, and pushing them to the limit.You can read more details in this Subjugator and Thol the Invincible article.

Subjugator and Thol introduce new legendaries, Firmware, and a new Pearlescent that can all be farmed as dedicated drops. Similar to Bloomreaper, you’ll only receive some of the gear if you can complete the fight in a short-enough time and at a high-enough difficulty level. Depending on your completion time, the Bronze, Silver, Gold, or Platinum-tier rewards you get from this chest will be increasingly powerful. You'll be able to clearly see how much time you have left to hit a certain tier via your mission tracker.

Ordonite Corruption Spreads Across Kairos 

Ordonite corruption is spreading throughout Kairos, and as a result, new treasures await Vault Hunters, including new Pearlescent weaponry! Fight your way through the enemies of Kairos, open each and every container, and dig your way through piles of trash in search of these new Pearls. They can be anywhere! 
Shared Progression Toggle

    As a refresher, Shared Character Progression was added in the 1.5 Update and allowed progression for Collectibles, Activities, Map Progress, SDU tokens, Vault Powers, Vehicle skins, and Hover Drives across all of your Vault Hunters. 
    We've heard the community's desire for Shared Character Progression to be optional so that starting fresh with a new Vault Hunter is still possible. This update makes this toggle available, starting today, when creating a new Vault Hunter. Thanks for your patience as we worked to make sure this feature accommodated players no matter their preference of playstyle!
    This new Shared Progression Toggle will be available when creating a New Character, after you select your difficulty. Please note that this setting cannot be changed after the character creation process is completed. 
    You can see (via tooltip) if a character has Shared Progression active when selecting the save you’d like to play.

C4SH the Rogue

We are giving C4SH a big tune-up! This update helps with skill tracking, improves how your cards target enemies, and makes sure your abilities scale into the late game. We also rebalanced a few skill combinations so your combat numbers stay powerful without breaking the game's challenge.
Skills
Infinite Bleed C4SH 

We found that several skills players have been pairing with the Gamer Class Mod led to situations where enemies bled out significantly faster than we intended. There were multiple causes for this, and after taking a close look, we’re introducing updates that will result in the Gamer Class Mod functioning more along the original lines we intended. Specifically:

    Can’t Stop Winning: Triggers specifically from Gun and Action Skill damage, and applies its Critical Hit chance exclusively to Action Skills 
    Sounds of Rain: Applies its Critical Hit chance to Action Skills
    Call: Excludes Status Effect damage from scoring Critical Hits 

 Roll the Bones - Green Tree

    Serendipity: Healing missiles from Serendipity heal C4SH if you kill an enemy while driving a vehicle
    Tooth and Nail: Applies its bonus melee damage whenever you fire the Maliwan Shotgun Mantra 

Luck of the Draw - Blue Tree

    Sleight of Hand: 
        Magic Missile Cards hit Splice enemies that have lost their upper bodies

    Sweeten the Pot: Windfall grants an extra random element when the Color Spray Card triggers more than 10 times
    C4SH Game: Allows Fortune bonuses to scale higher, rewarding you for earning bigger Fortune stacks
    Ace in the Hole: This is no longer a Fortune skill; the Gun Damage bonus is based on the number of stacks, rather than the Fortune ratio, and we’ve updated the skill’s description appropriately
    Bet on Red: Vampiric Touch Cards kill standard enemies instantly and deal critical damage against Badasses in Windfall; these cards also leave Ordonite Crystals attached to enemies intact 
    Payout: This has been updated so that it buffs all active Fortune skills
    Furious Fingers: Adds charges when Windfall is triggered and removes them when the skill is inactive; please be aware that the charges will appear on the Action Skill icon even when the skill is not active

Daedalus

    Bod Shotgun: 
        Dev Note: We’ve been looking at this piece of gear for some time now. Given that this gun simultaneously counts as an assault rifle, pistol, shotgun, sniper rifle, and SMG, we saw it quintuple dipping from bonuses derived from generic Gun Damage. We decided not to change that this gun can still be counted as each of those weapon types simultaneously, but we did elect to reduce how the Bod scales with generic Gun Damage.
        The Bod’s damage scaling from Gun Damage will decrease by about 60%
